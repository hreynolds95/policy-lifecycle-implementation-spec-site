Policy Development Lifecycle — AI Automation Opportunities
Project Outline & Scope Definition
Prepared: March 18, 2026 Source of Truth: Compliance Policy on Policies (PoP) — Block, Inc. Purpose: Define discrete automation workstreams that can streamline the compliance policy development lifecycle while preserving required human judgment, approval authority, and regulatory integrity.
Policy Baseline Control: This plan is implemented against the current approved PoP baseline. Any PoP draft comments, bracketed notes, or pending governance updates are treated as proposed changes until formally approved. Automation rules that depend on such items must be configuration-driven and versioned.


________________


1. Executive Summary
The Compliance Policy on Policies (PoP) establishes a rigorous four-tollgate lifecycle for developing, reviewing, approving, and publishing compliance documents. While this framework is essential for regulatory integrity, many of its checkpoints involve repetitive, pattern-based validations that are strong candidates for AI-assisted automation.


This project outline identifies six automation workstreams mapped directly to PoP requirements. Each workstream defines scope, success criteria, AI capabilities to leverage, what remains human-only, and estimated impact. The goal is not to replace human judgment but to accelerate cycle time, reduce manual error, and create consistent quality baselines so that human reviewers can focus on substantive regulatory and strategic decisions.

2. Current State: Policy Lifecycle Process Map
2.1 Tollgate Structure (from PoP)
Tollgate #1 — Intake Phase


* Owner initiates new document or off-cycle change request
* Regulatory requirements and existing documentation assessed
* Conflict checks with current inventory (Go/Policy, LogicGate SOR)
* Document strategy decision (new vs. expand existing)
* Compliance Domain alignment confirmed
* Template selection
* Policy Team notification to proceed


Tollgate #2 — Revision Phase


* Owner/Delegate drafts or revises document using approved template
* Stakeholder reviewers consulted and feedback documented
* Redlines and/or summary of changes prepared
* Roles and responsibilities reviewed with stakeholders
* Regulatory content updates reviewed with Legal Counsel as needed
* Supplemental materials (Standards, Procedures) identified
* Track changes and clean copy submitted to Policy Team


Tollgate #3 — Quality Control Phase


* Policy Team conducts offline QC review (18-item checklist)
* Material changes tracked via redline document
* Owner reviews and approves Policy Team's proposed changes
* Post-QC checklist (10-item) completed
* Approval framework compliance verified
* SOR and Go/Policy alignment confirmed


Tollgate #4 — Approvals/Publication Phase


* Clean and redline copies submitted to Approvers
* Approval per Compliance Document Approval Matrix (Tier 1/2/3)
* Written approvals documented with digital audit trail
* Policy Team publishes to LogicGate SOR and Go/Policy
* Communication via monthly updates, leadership reporting, stakeholder notifications
* Annual review date confirmed and documented
2.2 Key Roles in the Lifecycle
* Document Owner — Accountable for full lifecycle; content attestation
* Delegate — Drafting and stakeholder coordination on behalf of Owner
* Stakeholder Reviewer — Subject matter input and sign-off
* Policy Team — Governance, QC, publication, inventory management
* Compliance Leadership (C-Core / C-Core+1) — Final approval for Tier 3; escalation authority
* Legal Counsel — Regulatory interpretation and guidance
* Approver — Per Compliance Document Approval Matrix (Leadership, Committee, Board)

9. Cross-Cutting Considerations
9.1 Integration Architecture
All workstreams should integrate with:


* LogicGate — System of record for compliance documents, exceptions, lifecycle management
* Google Docs — Primary drafting and collaboration platform
* Go/Policy — Staff-facing publication platform
* Slack — Communication and notification channel
* Block Entity Registry — Authoritative source for entity names and hierarchy
9.2 Confidence Scoring Framework
Every automated check should return:


* Result: Pass / Fail / Needs Review
* Confidence: High (>90%) / Medium (70–90%) / Low (<70%)
* Evidence: Specific text, section, or data point supporting the finding
* PoP Reference: The specific PoP section or checklist item being validated
* Override Capability: Human reviewer can override with documented rationale
9.3 Audit Trail Requirements
Per PoP requirements, all automation must maintain:


* Complete record of automated checks performed and results
* Human override decisions and rationale
* Timestamp and user attribution for all actions
* Retention per Block's Data Classification and Handling Policy or applicable regulatory requirements (whichever is longer)
9.4 Risk Guardrails
* No autonomous approvals — AI assists and recommends; humans approve
* No autonomous publication — AI prepares; Policy Team publishes
* Regulatory content changes always flagged for human review — even if classified as Immaterial by AI
* Confidence thresholds — Items below Medium confidence automatically routed to human review
* Model drift monitoring — Regular accuracy audits against human reviewer decisions
