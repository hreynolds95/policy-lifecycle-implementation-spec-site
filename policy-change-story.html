Narrative Example

A regulatory change requires a material policy update.

The example below follows a policy owner updating an existing policy after a regulatory requirement changes. The same pattern also applies to standard updates, annual refreshes, and policy changes that require tiered approval under the PoP.

Scenario

What the system needs to prove

Business trigger A regulation changes.

The policy owner needs to update requirements, notify affected stakeholders, and retain evidence.

Governance need PoP tollgates still apply.

The change must pass intake, revision, QC, and approval before it becomes authoritative.

Audit need The final PDF must trace to source.

Every approved output should connect back to the source file, reviewers, checks, and merge commit.

Change Path

How the policy change moves through the DMS

01 Intake request Owner submits the change need.

The request captures document ID, change driver, regulatory implication, target date, and expected approval tier.

Creates: intake issue or request record.
02 Scope triage Policy Team confirms governance path.

The team validates that the change applies to a policy or standard and identifies required PoP tollgates.

Creates: routing labels and checklist requirements.
03 Draft branch Owner edits the source document.

The policy content changes in Markdown while the approved source record remains protected.

Creates: controlled draft workspace.
04 Change package Pull request opens for review.

The PR shows the redline, metadata changes, reviewer comments, required checks, and approval routing.

Creates: formal review and approval record.
05 QC gate Automated and human checks run.

Metadata, template structure, formatting, required fields, and publication readiness are validated.

Creates: workflow logs and QC checklist evidence.
06 Approval and merge Required approvers sign off.

Branch protection blocks merge until required reviewers and checks satisfy the configured tier path.

Creates: approved commit in the source of truth.
07 Publish and notify The approved source generates outputs.

The system publishes the staff-facing page or PDF, records the audit event, and triggers awareness communications.

Creates: published output, notification record, and audit log.
Evidence Created

What exists after the change is complete

Source Approved Markdown file

The exact document content that became authoritative.

Review Pull request record

Comments, redlines, required reviewers, approvals, and resolved feedback.

Controls Workflow and QC results

Automated checks and checklist outcomes showing control operation.

Publication PDF or portal output

The readable version employees use, tied back to the commit that produced it.

Awareness Communication record

Evidence that relevant stakeholders were notified of the material change.

Audit Evidence register entry

The retained event linking version, approvers, output, notification, and source commit.

Role View

Who interacts with the change

Policy owner

Raises the change, edits source content, responds to reviewer feedback, and confirms stakeholder impact.

Policy Team

Confirms governance path, performs QC, validates readiness, and controls publication handoff.

Approvers

Review the change package, resolve questions, and approve only when required evidence is complete.

Employees

Consume the published policy through the portal or PDF without needing to understand GitHub workflow details.
